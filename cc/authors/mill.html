Life and Work

John Stuart Mill was a political theorist, economist and a well known british philosopher. Mill's commitment to individual freedom and utilitarianism formed the cornerstone of his philosophy. A work foundational to his beliefs, "On Liberty," (1859), argued for the importance of personal autonomy and limits on state interference in lives of individuals. He centered his argument of the societal benefits of giving individuals wider autonomy. Additionally, his work "Utilitarianism," (1861), explored the ethical theory of utilitarianism, which focused on the concept of 'utility' meaning that actions should increase utility by as much as possible (i.e. doing the greatest good for the greatest amount of people) both these works are cornerstones for the beliefs that Mill held throughout his life.

Selected Work by Author

On Liberty, Utilitarianism & Other Essays (1859/1861)

Selected Quote from the Work
