Friedrich Nietzsche

Quick Facts

  • Born on October 15 1844
  • Youngest Person to hold the Chair of Classical Philology at the University of Basel
  • Although he's labeled as a "German Philosopher" he never held German citizenship
  • Died on August 25 1900

Life and Work

Friedrich Nietzsche was a German philosopher whose work undeniably altered Western philosophy. One of Nietzsche's most notable works is "On the Genealogy of Morals" (1887). Here, Nietzsche traces the roots of morality focusing on the transition from the usage of "good" and "bad" to "good" and "evil". While doing so, Nietzsche focuses on the effect morality has on the human condition. The ideas discussed in this work are some of Nietzsche's most profound.

Selected Work by Author

On the Genealogy of Morals & Ecce Homo (1887)

Selected Quote from the Work

"The slave revolt in morality begins when 'ressentiment' itself becomes creative and gives birth to values."
